After a hail storm, one of the first questions vehicle owners ask is: **"Will my insurance cover this?"** The short answer is yes — if you have comprehensive coverage. Here's exactly how to navigate the process from start to finish.

## Does insurance cover hail damage?

Hail damage falls under **comprehensive coverage** (sometimes called "other than collision" coverage). This is the portion of your auto insurance policy that covers damage from events outside your control — weather, theft, vandalism, and animal strikes.

**Key facts:**
- Comprehensive coverage is required to file a hail claim
- Hail claims are **not considered at-fault** — they're classified as acts of nature
- Filing a hail claim typically **does not increase your premiums**
- You'll pay your comprehensive deductible (commonly $250–$1,000)

If you only carry liability insurance, hail damage is unfortunately not covered.

## Step-by-step: filing your hail damage claim

### 1. Document the damage immediately

Before anything else, take thorough photos:

- **Overall shots** of the vehicle from all four sides
- **Close-up shots** of individual dents — shoot at an angle so dents cast shadows
- **Photos of the roof and hood** from above if possible
- **Note the date, time, and location** of the storm

Good documentation speeds up the claims process significantly.

### 2. Contact your insurance company

Call your insurer or file a claim through their app or website. You'll need:

- Your policy number
- Date and location of the storm
- Description of the damage
- Your photos

Most insurers assign a claim number within 24 hours.

Before an adjuster visits, it helps to have an independent estimate from a PDR specialist. This gives you a baseline to compare against the insurance company's assessment.

### 4. Meet the insurance adjuster

Your insurer will send an adjuster to inspect the vehicle. They'll count dents, note affected panels, and write an estimate. A few tips:

- **Be present during the inspection** so you can point out damage they might miss
- **Have your independent estimate ready** for comparison
- **Ask about supplement claims** — if additional damage is found during repair, a supplement can be filed for the difference

### 5. Choose your repair shop

You always have the **right to choose your own repair provider** — regardless of what your insurance company recommends. Insurers may suggest preferred shops, but they cannot require you to use one.

## Common questions about hail damage claims

### Will my rates go up?

In most states, **comprehensive claims do not affect your premiums** because the damage wasn't caused by your driving. However, policies vary — check with your agent to confirm.

### What if the damage exceeds my car's value?

If repair costs exceed a certain percentage of your vehicle's value (usually 70–80%), the insurer may declare it a **total loss**. In this case, they'll offer you the vehicle's actual cash value. You can negotiate this amount if you believe it's too low.

### Can I keep the insurance payout and not repair the car?

Technically yes — you can accept the payout and drive with the dents. However, unrepaired hail damage lowers resale value significantly and may cause rust over time. We strongly recommend getting the repair done.

### What if I don't have comprehensive coverage?
